---
title: "The Mornings I Still Miss"
description: "Small memories from the lockdown days that I still carry with me."
pubDate: "Jul 14 2026"
---

Some memories in life are very simple, but they stay in our hearts forever. For me, those memories come from the COVID-19 lockdown days in Sri Lanka.

During that time the whole country was closed. Schools were closed, roads were empty, and people stayed inside their homes. Many people remember that time as stressful and difficult, but my experience was a little different.

I live in a village in Kurunegala District(Bamunapotha), so life during the lockdown was quiet and slow. Every day I woke up around 4-5 AM. My alarm was the song “Runaway” by Aurora. In the early morning silence, when that song started playing, I felt completely calm and peaceful

After waking up, I started watching my A/L online class recordings. At that time I was not very active on social media, so most of my day was simple and focused. I still remember watching the Operating System Paging lesson on Pubudu Wijekoon sir's IT class. I spent time learning, watching lectures, and also learning to build things on the internet.

Once the classes and other tasks were done, I usually listened to the Derana Aruna morning program. They talked about the day's news and the latest COVID-19 updates in Sri Lanka. I still remember how Army Commander General Shavendra Silva used to join the program during that time to give updates on the COVID-19 situation. That was one of the things I looked forward to as well. Listening to those updates became part of my daily routine during the lockdown.

In front of our house, we have a small coconut land. Sometimes we went there to do a bit of planting vegetables like "nai kochchi", "karawila" and "bandakka". It was simple work, but there was something satisfying about spending time on the land and watching things grow.

At the time, I didn't realize how special those days were. It just felt like normal life. But looking back now, I understand how meaningful and simple those quiet moments.

Today I live in a crowded city for my higher studies. There are vehicles everywhere, constant noise, buildings, and concrete. Life moves fast here, and sometimes it feels uncomfortable compared to the peaceful village mornings I once had. But I know I need to be here for now, to build my future :|

That is why I still remember those lockdown days :)

Maybe I cannot go back to those exact days again. Life always moves forward. But those memories remind me that happiness is often found in the simplest moments.
